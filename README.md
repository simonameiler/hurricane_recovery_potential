# Hurricane recovery potential
Understanding post-disaster recovery dynamics is crucial for enhancing community resilience in the face of increasing climate-related hazards. While much attention has been paid to the immediate impacts of hurricanes, less is known about how local recovery unfolds over time, especially across diverse socio-economic and hazard contexts. Building permit activity offers a valuable proxy for measuring reconstruction efforts, providing insight into the pace and scale of recovery at the community level.
In this study, we aim to assess post-disaster recovery potential across regions exposed to hurricanes in the United States. Specifically, we ask: </br>
1. How does local post-disaster recovery potential, as proxied by building permit activity, vary across regions exposed to hurricanes in the USA?
2. Which regions exhibit low recovery potential relative to their hurricane risk, and how could this inform resilience building and disaster recovery planning?

This project explores integrating seismic resilience modeling with climate risk assessment by leveraging two open-source tools: [Pyrecodes](https://nikolablagojevic.github.io/pyrecodes/html/usage/what_is_pyrecodes.html), which simulates regional recovery and disaster resilience in the built environment, and [CLIMADA](https://github.com/CLIMADA-project/climada_python), a probabilistic climate risk modeling platform. By combining these frameworks, we aim to develop a more comprehensive approach to quantifying climate resilience, capturing not only the direct impacts of extreme events but also the mechanisms that drive long-term recovery.
